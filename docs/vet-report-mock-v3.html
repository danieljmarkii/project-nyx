Appendix F — How to read this report

Owner-reported
Every entry was logged by the owner on a phone. This is a record of what the owner observed, not a clinical examination, and contains no diagnosis or treatment recommendation.
Range
Scoped to since the last veterinary visit (May 1, 2026). The owner can change the range when generating the report; a custom (hand-picked) window discloses the count of symptom events that fall outside it, so nothing is cropped to a good week.
Denominators
Counts are shown over their window and the days logged (e.g. “9 episodes / 52 d”), so a count is never read without knowing how long and how completely it was tracked.
Logging coverage
Something was logged on 48 of 52 days; nothing on May 13, May 27, Jun 11, or Jun 18. Absence of an entry is not evidence a symptom did not occur.
Severity
Owner-reported on a 1–5 scale, per event in appendix A. It is intentionally never averaged into a headline figure; trend is read from frequency.
Time confidence
seen witnessed (exact time) · est an estimated time · range found later; the window it occurred in is shown, not the time it was noticed.
Photo analysis
For photographed incidents, Nyx reads structured fields from the photo (colour, contents, blood, foreign material). These are owner-reviewable and aggregated descriptively (appendix A, page 1). They never carry a diagnosis or a single-incident verdict, and a clear photo is never an all-clear.
Blood & foreign material
Reported only when seen in an incident — never as a “0 of N” count, because absence in a photo cannot exclude bleeding (digested blood photographs poorly) and these are AI reads. A flagged incident leads the safety band and its photo is offered as a permanent record copy.
Weight
Owner home-scale weigh-ins, shown as a trend rather than a single point. Descriptive context, never a diagnosis or an alarm; body condition is not assessed here.
Photos & verify
Everyday incident photos are viewable only while this link is live and are not in the printed copy; a safety-flagged photo is offered as a permanent copy for the record. The footer link and QR point to a public verification page and carry no private key — the report opens only through the owner-shared, expiring, revocable link.
Associations
Any timing relationship is reported as co-occurrence with counts for the clinician to weigh. Nothing in this report asserts that a food caused a symptom.
Deleted entries
Entries the owner deleted are excluded. Every figure is computed over exactly the events listed in appendices A–B — nothing is counted that is not shown.
