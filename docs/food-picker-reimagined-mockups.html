What if the food picker didn't have to be a picker?

Round 1 (docs/food-picker-findability-mockups.html, PR #351) tuned the existing list: rotation shelf, pinned search, photos. This round takes the brief you actually gave — significant resources, reimagine it — and lets the squad question the premise.

The premise worth questioning: a picker assumes the owner arrives knowing nothing. But by week two, Nyx knows the household's feeding rhythm better than any list can express. Every direction below spends that knowledge differently.

Grounded in docs/logging-capture-discovery.md (2026-07-10) · Principles 1–2 · the capture-fidelity north star Squad full product team, conflicts surfaced inline Date 2026-07-15

The squad's shared frame before diverging

Three constraints from the July-10 logging-friction discovery bind every direction here — they're what keeps blue-sky from becoming off-brand:

  • Nothing is ever assumed into existence. A prediction, a schedule slot, a scan — all of them lower the cost of confirming; none may record an event from silence (the B-156 G1 rule, generalized). A slot nobody answered renders as a gap, not a meal.
  • Capture fidelity, not logging frequency. A fabricated meal poisons the correlation engine's control windows. Every new lane carries logged_via provenance (B-289) so the engine and the vet report always know how a record was born.
  • Answering beats initiating. The discovery's core behavioral finding. The picker today is an initiation surface; the deepest reimagining flips it to a response surface — which is also what makes it compose with the B-288 notification pilot instead of competing with it.

The prediction is deterministic and computed on-device: per-pet food frequencies inside the current learned time window (trailing 30 days). No LLM, no server call, works offline.
  • 1One unmistakable card. The photo is large precisely so a lazy confirm is hard to get wrong — you can't tap "yes" to the salmon can while holding the turkey can without noticing. The "why" line shows the receipts (11 of 14 mornings), never "Nyx's favorite."
  • 2Never auto-logged, never pre-armed on ambiguity. If the window's history is mixed (no food ≥60% of recent logs in this window), the hero collapses to the "Also likely" row and the picker behaves like today. Silence records nothing.
  • 3The full picker survives underneath — search, library, add-new. The prediction is a layer, not a replacement, so the worst case is exactly today's screen.
  • 4Provenance travels: logged_via='predicted_confirm' on every record born here, so the engine can weight it and the funnel can measure it.

The can is already in your hand when you feed. Point the camera at it: barcode decodes on-device (free, offline), matches upc_barcode in the library, meal logs. Unknown package? The same viewfinder flows straight into the existing capture-and-extract pipeline — one flow for known and unknown foods, instead of today's fork between "pick from list" and "snap a new food."

Scan is a lane, not the whole picker — the shelf and library stay one swipe below for staples, where a tap will always beat a scan.
  • 1Barcode first, on-device, offline, $0 per scan. upc_barcode has been in the schema (indexed, unique) since the food-library migration — it's been waiting for exactly this. Photo-match against library photos is the fallback for barcode-less packages; a perceptual-hash spike decides on-device vs Edge.
  • 2Match card confirms before logging — never scan-and-silently-log. Wrong match is one tap from the library below.
  • 3Unknown packages merge the two flows. Today "log a known food" and "add a new food" are different doors; here the viewfinder is one door and the system sorts out which case you're in.
DesignerThe discovery doc's environmental-cue principle, applied in-app: the strongest cue is the physical object, and this makes the object itself the affordance. It also finally gives the barcode-encouraged capture step (§4.2 of the redesign) a daily payoff — owners who snapped barcodes get rewarded every meal.
Jordan (dog owner)Skeptical for staples: aiming a camera one-handed with a dog underfoot loses to tapping a Recent tile every time. Where it wins: the new bag, the rotation of prescription cans that all look alike, the pet-sitter week.
Dr. ChenQuietly the strongest data-quality direction: a barcode match is ground-truth food identity — no mis-tap, no duplicate, exact SKU. Every scan-logged meal hardens the exposure record the report leans on.
Product OwnerComposes with, and partly delivers, B-009 dedup phase 1: a scan that hits an existing UPC cannot create a duplicate — match-and-steer by construction.
Trust & SafetyCamera frames for matching stay on-device unless the food is new (then the existing upload consent applies). No always-on camera; viewfinder only while the lane is open.

Build: ~2–3 PRs (barcode decode + UPC match lane → unknown-package merge into food-capture → photo-match spike, optional). No schema. Note: barcode-only scan was explicitly deferred post-MVP in the May-2026 requirements — promoting it is a scope call for you.

R3The day board — feeding is a ritual, not a search

The deepest reimagining — the picker stops being a picker New schema — feeding schedule as a first-class object Gated on the B-288 pilot by the discovery's own sequencing

Owners don't select from a catalog three times a day — they run a routine. R3 makes the routine the surface: today's feeding board, slots pre-staged with the usual food, each slot one tap to confirm when it happens. The board, the B-288 notification, the future widget, and the reconciliation card become four views of the same object.

  • 1Slots, not lists. Breakfast/dinner declared once at setup (or learned, then confirmed) — the implementation-intention moment from the discovery: configuring the schedule is forming the habit plan.
  • 2Confirm, swap, or leave open. The staged food is R1's prediction wearing a schedule; "Something else" drops into the classic picker. Silence = a gap, honestly rendered.
  • 3Treats stay slot-less — they're the spontaneous class from the taxonomy; the board gives them a counter, not a schedule, and the widget/NFC lanes (B-290/291) remain their real fix.
  • 4Grazing gets its honest home: the free-choice row folds B-040 and the B-341 grazer-deferred-intake mechanic into the board's day-close ritual instead of bolting them onto a picker they never fit.
Persona conflict — PM callDesigner: this is the honest end-state — every other direction is an approximation of it, and it's the only one that makes "scheduled-slot coverage" (the discovery's north-star proxy) a thing the owner can see. Dir. of Engineering: it's also the only one with real structural cost — a feeding_schedules table (own migration PR, pet_id + RLS, local mirror, sync), plus a new Home-adjacent surface to maintain. Building it before B-288's pilot proves the answering-beats-initiating hypothesis inverts the discovery's own sequencing. Both are right; the resolution is sequencing, and that's yours.
Data ScientistThe quiet prize: declared slots turn capture fidelity from unmeasurable into a real denominator — meals logged ÷ slots declared. That single number is what the week-6 retention conversation, the B-047 instrumentation plan, and the vet report's "completeness" line all want.
QABiggest state machine of the four (slot × confirmed/unanswered/swapped/skipped × grazer × multi-pet × partner writes). Needs its own spec before a line of code.

Build: a track, not a PR — spec → schema migration → board UI → picker integration → B-288 wiring. Sequence after the pilot per the discovery's own §6 logic.
