Appendix F — How to read this report

Safety flags
Shown only when present, above the fold. They escalate on the presence of a concern (reduced intake, possible blood, worsening) and are owner-reported, not a diagnosis. Absence of a flag is never shown as an “all clear.”
Intake
Where the owner logs meals, a declined or barely-touched meal is recorded as a possible health signal — never “picky.” Absence of a meal log is not read as “didn’t eat.”
Blood & foreign material
Reported only when seen in an incident — never as a “0 of N” count. A flagged incident leads the safety band and its photo is retained as a permanent record copy. A photo cannot exclude bleeding.
Weight
Owner home-scale weigh-ins, shown as a trend. Descriptive, not a diagnosis; in a cat off food, weight loss is the trajectory that matters.
Denominators
Every count carries its window and days logged, so a count is never read without knowing how completely it was tracked.
Time confidence
seen witnessed · est estimated · range found later; the window it occurred in is shown, not the time noticed.
Photos & verify
Everyday photos are viewable only while this link is live and are not in the printed copy; a safety-flagged photo is retained permanently for the record. The footer link and QR carry no private key.
