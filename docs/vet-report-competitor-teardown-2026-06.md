## 1. Executive summary — the findings that should shape our report

**① The single most important meta-finding: almost nobody publishes a rendered artifact of their vet report.** Across ~25 products, **not one** AI-summary vendor (Dutch, CompanAIn, PerkyPet, Modern Animal, Digitail, TTcare) exposes a field-level schema or a rendered sample of its vet-facing output — every contents list is *marketed inputs*, never *verified report fields*. The only players with genuinely itemizable structured output are **IDEXX** (lab interpretation) and the **hardware biometric** players (PetPace, Invoxia, Maven). **Implication:** our planned field-level itemization — counts *with denominators*, occurred-vs-logged timestamps, a computed adherence line, an appendix every page-1 number traces to — is **more concrete than anything any competitor has shown publicly.** That specificity is a differentiator, not table stakes.

**② "Export a PDF to your vet" is now table stakes; "synthesize a clinical answer" is not.** The entire indie crop (11pets, Vet Record, Vettie, Petfetti, Everkin, PetVitality, PetDocs, PetNoter, DogLog, PokiPaw) ships a static, owner-generated **record dump** — vaccines, meds, weight, vet-visit history, symptom log, microchip/insurance — organized by category or date. **None computes a denominatored rate, a synthesized trend, a correlation, or any timestamp/intake honesty.** Confirmed at field level: the "synthesize, don't dump" thesis is an empirical gap, not a taste preference.

**③ The word "report" hides three structurally different things — and Nyx's quadrant is empty.**
- **Clinic-EHR / vet-authored (walled):** Modern Animal/Chewy, Digitail, IDEXX, TTcare Vet — the artifact is generated by the *clinic's* software from clinic data (or owner intake that lands in the clinic record). Only reaches a vet if *that* clinic runs *that* software. The owner never holds a portable document.
- **Hardware single-domain (portable but narrow):** PetPace (biometrics/pain/seizure), Invoxia (cardiac/respiratory), Maven (vitals/activity), Fi (activity telemetry) — owner-portable, downloadable, works with any vet, **but vitals-only** (no diet, no symptom-frequency-with-denominators, no med adherence).
- **Owner-curated dump (portable but unsynthesized):** Zoetis The Pack + the indie crop.
- **Nyx's slot is the empty quadrant: owner-assembled, owner-portable, broad-spectrum (signalment + symptom counts w/ denominators + WSAVA-grade diet + meds/adherence + provenance appendix), free, under n=1 safety.** No one occupies it. CompanAIn is the nearest conceptual neighbor and is **walled behind a vet login** (see ⑤).

**④ The denominator is the defensible field.** No competitor's vet output carries **per-symptom frequency counts with explicit denominators** ("9 episodes / 52 days, logged 48 of 52"). The closest structured analogs are IDEXX's lab-side IRIS staging / risk-with-time-horizon and the hardware "deviation-from-personal-baseline" flags — both numeric, neither an owner-logged behavioral *rate*. Severity/confidence grading is conspicuously **unpublished** even where claimed (TTcare, PetPace pain score).

**⑤ CompanAIn is the real structural twin — and it makes the mistake the send-ahead research says is fatal.** CompanAIn (vet portal live 2026-06-23) is owner-built → owner invites *their own* outside vet → free portal, explicitly meant to be opened *before* the appointment. But it requires the **vet to log into a portal** — and the #1 documented failure of patient-shared data in human medicine is "the clinician had to log into another system." **Nyx's no-account share-token + forwardable PDF is the right call; do not require a vet login.**

**⑥ "Send the report to the vet ahead of time" is essentially unsolved owner-side — this is open competitive space, but it's a delivery/workflow problem, not a report-generation one.** Every consumer tracker (Nyx included today) stops at "owner emails the PDF themselves." The only true pre-visit-into-the-record systems are **clinic-owned** (Digitail, PetDesk) an owner can't trigger for an arbitrary vet. Human-medicine evidence is sobering: ~**half of pre-visit submissions go unread**, and clinician review tends to make visits **longer, not shorter** (+34% / +6.8 min RCT). **Sell it as "your vet walks in already knowing the story," never "reclaim the 15 minutes."** (Full treatment + the captured backlog item: §8.)

**⑦ Competitors are actively on the wrong side of our guardrails — at the report layer specifically.** Zoetis lets the owner **cherry-pick what's in the report** (no out-of-window disclosure — exactly the risk our spec §6 cherry-pick guard closes). IDEXX's client summary leans on **"normal preventive-screening reassurance"** (n=1 absence-reassurance). PerkyPet **paywalls vet data-sharing at $12.95/mo/pet** (anti-Pets > $). Each is a field-level confirmation of a decision our spec already made.

> **One-sentence answer to the PM's question:** *Every competitor's vet report is either a walled clinic artifact, a narrow hardware-vitals export, or an owner-curated record dump — and not one publicly shows a rendered field list, computes a denominatored symptom rate, distinguishes witnessed-vs-found timestamps, or carries an honest medication-adherence line; the real veterinary documents (WSAVA diet form, SOAP, referral letter, discharge summary) define the data vocabulary, and Nyx's planned report is the only one aiming to deliver the owner-reportable half of that vocabulary, synthesized and portable, under n=1 safety.*

## 2. The canonical clinical data-point taxonomy (the spine)

Every competitor and our own report maps onto this. Derived from the **real** veterinary documents (WSAVA Short Diet History Form, the veterinary SOAP note, the specialist referral letter, the PIMS discharge/visit summary) plus the human-medicine After-Visit Summary. The **Owner-side?** column is the bright line Nyx lives on: **Nyx populates the owner-reportable categories and deliberately stops before assessment / plan / diagnosis.**

| # | Category | The data-point vocabulary | Owner-side? (Nyx can populate) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Signalment / patient identity** | name, species, breed, age/DOB, sex & neuter, weight, color/markings, **microchip** | **Yes** |
| 2 | **Owner & care-team identity** | owner name/contact; clinic, attending/referring vet | **Yes** (owner half) |
| 3 | **Presenting / chief complaint** | reason for visit, problem in owner's words | **Yes** |
| 4 | **History of presenting complaint** | onset, duration, progression, **FREQUENCY**; response to prior tx | **Yes** — *Nyx's wedge* |
| 5 | **Symptom log / frequency & character** | vomiting, diarrhea/stool consistency, cough, appetite, lethargy — **as counts/rates over time** | **Yes** — *quantified frequency = the differentiator* |
| 6 | **Diet history (WSAVA-grade)** | brand · product · form · amount · frequency · duration for **main food + treats + snacks + table food + dental chews + rawhides + foods-used-to-give-meds + "other"**; **supplements**; who feeds; access to other food; water | **Yes** — *WSAVA hands this to the owner* |
| 7 | **Medications** | drug (generic+brand) · dose · route · frequency · duration · with/without food; **at-home adherence / missed doses**; adverse reactions | **Partial** — current meds + adherence; the *Rx decision* is vet-only |
| 8 | **Weight & body/muscle condition** | body weight (**trend**); BCS /9 (fat); MCS (muscle, palpation); ideal/target weight | **Split** — at-home weight = owner; **BCS/MCS = vet** |
| 9 | **Vital signs** | temp, HR/pulse, resp rate, mucous membranes, CRT, hydration | **No — vet-measured** |
| 10 | **Physical examination** | per-system exam (EENT, oral/dental, nodes, heart/lungs, abdomen, urogenital, MSK, skin, neuro) | **No — vet-only** |
| 11 | **Diagnostics & results** | CBC/chem, urinalysis, fecal, imaging, cytology/biopsy, cultures | **No** (owner can *attach* a copy) |
| 12 | **Assessment** | problem list, **differentials, diagnosis, prognosis**, reasoning | **No — vet-judgment; Nyx does NOT produce** |
| 13 | **Plan** | tx/Rx, procedures, client instructions, diet/activity, recheck, referral | **No — vet-directed; Nyx does NOT produce** |
| 14 | **Home-care & monitoring** | what to do at home; **"when to seek care"** | **No (vet-issued)** — Nyx *consumes* this to drive escalation |
| 15 | **Provenance / who-reported & when** | owner-reported(S) vs vet-measured(O) vs vet-judgment(A/P); **observation timestamp; witnessed vs found**; date/location | **Yes — and a Nyx strength** (B-010) |
| 16 | **Risk-factor screen (WSAVA checklist)** | GI signs, prior/ongoing disease, current meds/supplements, unconventional diet, treats >10% calories, housing; weight change; dental/skin; life stage | **Mostly yes** (inputs owner-observable; the *trigger* is the vet's) |
| 17 | **Attachments / enclosures** | prior records, lab/imaging files, photos, invoice, handouts | **Yes** (Nyx supports event attachments) |

**The load-bearing read:** real vet documents split cleanly — **1–8, 15–17 are owner-reportable** (history, diet, symptom *frequency*, meds-as-adhered, at-home weight, provenance, attachments); **9–14 are vet-measured/vet-judgment** (vitals, exam, diagnostics, assessment, plan), which Nyx deliberately does **not** generate. **Nyx's defensible territory is the entire Subjective layer + the owner-side of weight/meds — made rigorous, quantified, and time-stamped.** The AVS usability literature then sets the bar for *how* to present it: **curated & prioritized (not a feed), plain-language, action-first, accurate, safety-leads, auto-synthesized** — the same discipline our design principles + clinical guardrails already encode.

## 4. Per-competitor field-level teardown

Grouped by archetype. **Recurring caveat:** unless a row says "VERIFIED," the field list is the vendor's *marketed* contents — no rendered artifact was public. Full source URLs in the research transcripts; key ones inline.

### 4A. AI clinical summaries (vet-facing or owner→vet)

**Dutch — "vet-ready brief"** `[E marketed]`
- *Inputs synthesized:* medical history · prescriptions · owner-uploaded photos · behavioral assessments → "500+ unique data points." **The 500+ is an *ingest* count, not 500 printed fields.**
- *Pre-consult intake feeding it:* a few health-issue questions → (issue-dependent) a longer symptom questionnaire + photos.
- *Adjacent consult outputs (not the brief):* SOAP notes · brief client summary · treatment recommendations.
- *No published section list; no rendered brief exists publicly.* **Captive** — feeds Dutch's own vets inside Dutch's EMR; an owner cannot generate it for their independent vet (outside-vet record transfer is a manual support request).

**CompanAIn — "Unified Health Summary" (vet portal)** `[E marketed]` — *the structural twin*
- *Unified Health Summary sections:* Conditions · Treatments · Vaccinations · Medications · Diagnostics · Wellness trends.
- *Living Health Timeline:* health events · visits · test results · care activities, each "enriched with AI insights."
- *Health Monitoring & Alerts:* health flags · overdue care · abnormal results · **medication adherence** · emerging risk factors.
- *Shared AI Insights:* the vet can review/agree/disagree with owner-viewed AI observations + add clinical context.
- *Only verifiable numeric example:* gradual hematocrit decline 43%→38%→33%.
- *Delivery:* owner emails an invite → **free vet portal login**, revocable. Some portal features (lab interpretation, agree/disagree) sit on a "coming soon" page despite the 2026-06-23 launch → treat as possibly-roadmap. **No rendered summary public.**

**PerkyPet AI — "comprehensive EHR in advance"** `[MK, pre-launch]`
- *No branded artifact name; no field schema.* Marketed tracked inputs (from privacy policy + PR): pet name/species/breed/age · medical history · vaccination records · nutrition · behavior · activity · symptoms · medications · weight/diet.
- *Vet-delivery channel: undefined* (no portal/PDF/email/PIMS named). The marquee "sent to your vet in advance" is a **paid** ($12.95/mo) feature. Summer-2026 launch; nothing shipped to verify.

**Modern Animal / Chewy — "Patient Overview" + "Visit Summarization"** `[E exists / MK contents]`
- *Patient Overview (vet, pre-visit):* "synthesizes the patient's entire medical history into one snapshot" — **no section breakdown published.**
- *Visit Summarization (client, post-visit):* vet-reviewed client-friendly narrative — no field list.
- *Lab-work interpretation (vet).* **Vet-authored from clinic records, fully walled** (membership clinic). Wrong-facing for our wedge: owner-facing piece is *post*-visit.

**IDEXX DecisionIQ (VetConnect PLUS)** `[E — best-itemized in the set]`
- *Inputs:* species/age · CBC, chemistry, electrolytes, urinalysis, SDMA, 4Dx Plus · prior results for trending · clinician input.
- *Vet outputs:* trending graphs normalized across modalities · result **flags** (out-of-range) · **reference intervals** · **differential diagnoses** · **disease-risk indications with time horizons** (same-day / 6-mo / 18-mo) · **IRIS CKD staging** · patient-specific interpretive text + next-step test recs.
- *Owner output:* "client-friendly Diagnostic Result Summary" — esp. **normal-screening reassurance** (an n=1 absence-reassurance pattern to *avoid*). **Lab-only; clinic-gated.**

**Digitail — Tails AI intake → SOAP** `[E help-doc / MK trackers]`
- *Owner intake captured:* reason for visit · free-text symptom description (AI-expanded with dynamic follow-ups) · **current meds + dose + frequency** · urgency triage flag.
- *Output into record:* conversation summary → appointment internal notes + the SOAP section at exam open.
- *Profile summary pulls from:* SOAP fields · diagnoses · prescriptions · appointments · lab orders · vitals (weight, temp). **Walled to the Digitail clinic.** Announced chronic-disease trackers (diabetes/allergies/kidney) **unshipped**.

**TTcare Vet** `[E components / MK severity]`
- *Screens:* eyes · skin/torso · teeth · ears · paws (dog); eyes · teeth (cat) — 31 symptoms.
- *Vet report:* AI scan result (visual) · abnormal signs · **DDx list** · real-time **SOAP** record · caregiver instructions · lesion progression over time.
- *Severity/confidence grading: NOT published* (only a model-level "95% accuracy"). **Software-gated.**

### 4B. Hardware biometric reports (owner-portable, vitals-narrow)

**Maven Pet — "health report" (Wellbeing tab export, v1.27.0+)** `[E feature / A layout]`
- *Biometric trends (deterministic rollups):* resting **respiratory rate** (with a ~30 bpm optimal-range line — the most clinically-framed metric) · resting **heart rate** · **activity** (vs personalized goal) · **rest/sleep** (+ sleep score) · **scratching/head-shaking** · **water intake — dogs only**. All as day/week/month charts with daily average + range + personal baseline.
- *Owner-logged that can accompany:* symptoms · medications · behaviors · weekly check-ins · Journal calendar.
- *NOT tracked:* weight is owner-entered (not sensor); no steps/GPS/calorie/BCS/temp. *No rendered export public; no confirmed signalment block.* Separate **AI-Vet™** pushes anomaly alerts + 24h updates to *partner* clinics (PIMS-integrated, walled).

**PetPace V3.0 — "Share with Your Vet" portal + downloadable reports** `[E metrics]`
- *Vitals:* HR · **HRV→stress** · respiration · temperature.
- *AI scores:* **Pain Score** · stress · sleep · wellness index · fitness (numeric scale unpublished).
- *Behavior:* activity, posture, sleep quality, calorie burn.
- *Seizure/epilepsy module:* event log with timing/frequency/patterns · ~1-hr alert · treatment-effectiveness trend reports.
- *Alerts:* color-coded flags vs personal baseline · alert-history timeline · daily/weekly/monthly graphs. + GPS. **Owner-initiated portal share; downloadable.**

**Invoxia (Minitailz)** `[E metrics — best send-ahead framing]`
- *Cardiac:* resting HR (vs baseline) · HRV · **AFib/arrhythmia detection** · **HeartPrint** (Poincaré/Lorenz rhythm "fingerprint").
- *Respiratory:* resting/sleeping resp rate (**ACVIM-compliant** — the metric cardiologists use for CHF).
- *Activity context:* activity, rest/sleep, meal detection, restlessness, walking, calories.
- *Export:* **single-click PDF of all HR + RR curves + arrhythmia patterns to your own vet/cardiologist.** Value prop verbatim: "arrive at the consultation with data spanning several weeks the vet cannot get anywhere else." *PDF layout not published.*

**Fi — "Vet Health Report" (separate from Fi Intelligence)** `[E help-doc]`
- **Two distinct things:** (1) **Fi Intelligence** (the marketed "vet visit companion," ~2026-03) = a conversational chatbot over collar data + uploaded records — **no shareable artifact, output is ephemeral chat.** (2) **Vet Health Report** = a separate, **non-AI** PDF of collar **activity/behavior telemetry** (last 30 days + 52 weeks): steps/activity, rest/sleep, Series-3+ detected behaviors (scratching/licking/barking/eating/drinking).
- *NOT included:* no signalment, no symptom counts, no severity, no diet/med detail, no uploaded records. Owner downloads + shares manually. Dog-only.

### 4C. Owner-curated record-dump exports (portable, unsynthesized)

**Zoetis "The Pack" — owner-configurable "veterinary report"** `[E logs / MK report]` — *closest shipped owner-logging→report competitor*
- *Owner picks what to include* ("create a veterinary report and choose what all you want in it"). Candidate categories: diet · health conditions · records · **observations** (e.g. coughing, bowel movements) · medications + reminders · activity · sleep · appointments · vaccines · weight/wellness · **dated photos/videos**.
- *Configurability is the headline — and the hazard:* the owner cherry-picks categories, **with no out-of-window/cherry-pick disclosure** (the exact risk our spec §6 guard closes).
- *No rendered export public* → no verified per-symptom counts/denominators, severity scale, or appendix tables. Owner-as-courier (no send-ahead).

**The indie crop — common payload** `[E listings / A exact PDF]`
A static, owner-generated export — **almost always PDF, frequently a CSV companion**, delivered by email / AirDrop / "any app" / print / one-tap share — built from the same core: **vaccination records · medications · weight history · vet-visit history · symptom log · (very commonly) microchip + insurance + stored lab/document scans.** Standouts at field level:

## 7. The "send the report to the vet ahead of time" finding (PM's explicit interest)

**The capability is rare-to-nonexistent owner-side — and the reason it's unsolved is delivery + workflow, not report generation.**

**What exists today:**
- **Owner-as-courier (the default, incl. Nyx today):** generate a PDF/link, owner emails/AirDrops/hands it over. Zoetis, Fi, and the entire indie crop stop here. "Share with your vet" = "share it yourself."
- **Clinic-owned pre-visit-into-the-record (solved, but not ours to trigger):** **Digitail** (owner intake → SOAP record before exam) and **PetDesk/Vetstoria** (intake forms write back into the PIMS) genuinely land data in the vet's workflow before arrival — *but the clinic owns it;* an owner can't invoke it for an arbitrary vet.
- **Owner-initiated-to-own-vet (the nearest analog):** **CompanAIn** — owner email-invites their vet to a free portal, "opened in minutes before the appointment." Closest to the idea — but **requires the vet to log into a portal** (the fatal friction). Hardware players (**Invoxia, PetPace**) let owners export weeks of continuous data ahead of a consult — portable, but vitals-only.

**What human medicine teaches (the sobering part — use it to set expectations):**
- **Getting data to the clinician is the easy half; getting it read is the hard half.** In a primary-care RCT, 70% of patients submitted a pre-visit journal but only ~71% of those were *opened* by the provider — **~half read, and only with EHR integration.** Patients themselves doubt it's read.
- **Pre-visit data does NOT reliably shorten the visit.** The classic RCT of physician-reviewed pre-visit questionnaires found intervention visits **34% longer (+6.8 min)** — the time went into discussing surfaced concerns. Efficiency appears only when the data is *synthesized and EHR-integrated*.
- **What makes pre-visit data usable, not ignored:** EHR/workflow integration (never a second system to log into) · synthesis & prioritization (dashboards/thresholds, not raw logs) · actionability · accuracy · don't add clinician work.
- **Delivery friction is real:** vet clinics still receive outside docs by **email, fax, portal, hand-carried, scanned-into-PIMS**; inbound is a front-desk human bottleneck; 64% of vets report admin workload doubled. An unsynthesized inbound report is *more* work, not less.

**Recommendation (and the captured backlog item, B-196):**
- **Frame the value correctly:** "your vet walks in already knowing the story / a better-targeted visit," **never** "reclaim the 15 minutes." (The +6.8-min RCT means the shorter-visit pitch is falsifiable and would burn vet trust.)
- **v1 (buildable on top of Step 9, honest):** owner-initiated send to a **vet email the owner enters** + the clean PDF + the existing share-token link, optionally **scheduled** ("send Thursday; my appt is Friday"). This is *already ahead of the consumer-tracker pack* — **if and only if the artifact is genuinely synthesized** (which is exactly what Step 9 builds). Be candid in-product that reaching the vet's eyes depends on the clinic's inbox habits — we control artifact quality, not their workflow.
- **Hard constraint:** meet the vet where they already are (their inbox / a forwardable PDF / eventually a PIMS scan) — **never a Nyx vet login.** Land as *observation*, never diagnosis (already our posture).
- **Aspirational (the actual moat, multi-quarter, a BD/strategy play not a build task):** a vet-directory + **delivery-confirmation** layer, and eventually **PIMS write-in / partner integrations** (the Digitail/PetDesk model) so the report lands *in the record* before exam start — plus a Vetster-style "send even to a clinic that isn't a partner" fallback. **Flag as a PM/strategy decision.**
- **Composability:** gated on Step 9 (the report must exist first); rides the share-token infra and the B-023 "Share with my vet" bridge. It is a natural Phase-5 follow-on to the Step-9 plan.
