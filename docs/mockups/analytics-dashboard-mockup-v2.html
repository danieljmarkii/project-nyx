The direction trade-off — and a recommendation

All three carry the identical seeded cards, the inverted-colour polarity, and every §11 safety invariant. The only thing that changes is what greets you at the top — which sets the whole surface's personality.
DimensionA · Summary-ledB · Hero-stat-ledC · Calm uniform
What leadsThe AI narrativeThe safety-critical numberNothing — even weight
Answers “is she okay?”Via the sentenceInstantly, by the numberBy reading the list
Our differentiatorFront-and-centre (the AI summary moat)Present, secondaryDe-emphasised
Calmness (Principle 3)Warm, editorialOne bold stat, then calmMaximally calm
Safety-lead visibility (§11)Summary + first cardThe lead IS the heroOrder only, not weight
RiskComing-soon occupies the hero slot until PR 4“What leads on a calm week?” needs an answerFlat — under-surfaces the one thing that matters
Designer lean → A (summary-led), with B as the strong contender. A matches §7's intent — the AI summary is the thing Nyx is uniquely positioned to offer, and it keeps the safety card leading immediately below — so it's the most “us.” B is genuinely compelling for Jordan's literal question (“is she okay?”) and is worth a real look if we want the safety metric to dominate the glance; its open problem is what fills the hero on a week with no clear concern (the engine picks safety-first, but it must never become a gamified “score”). C is the safe fallback — the Bearable-style restraint we'd reach for if we ever worried the summary over-promises — but it spends our biggest differentiator to get there. My recommendation: build A, borrow B's “vs baseline” prominence into the lead card.
