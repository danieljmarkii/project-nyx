Lane A vs Lane B — the trade-off

Both lanes use the same detector and the same strings. The decision is purely about which surface the owner sees the observation on, and the two surfaces have opposite visibility profiles.
DimensionLane A — band-2 cardLane B — coverage lane
When it showsWhenever the observation is true — even alongside firing findingsOnly on no_pattern (nothing else fired)
Churn visibilityAlways — incl. when symptoms active & findings fire (its best case)Silent whenever a finding fires (churn needs active symptoms → most likely to be hidden)
FramingA behavior note sits among pet-state findings → risk of reading as a verdict / judgmentFramed as data coverage / “here's why I can't conclude” → never a pet-state claim
Card cap (§3.2)Counts against the ~4-card cap; can push a real finding downNo cost — lives on the otherwise-empty surface
Retention fitEnriches an already-rich surfaceEnriches the thin no_pattern surface B-053 exists to fix (Nyx falls there today)
Spec signal§2/§6 describe band-2 ranking & cap-subject§5.2's “never co-render with staple_washout” only makes sense in the coverage lane
Product-team lean → Lane B (coverage lane). These observations describe the owner's feeding/logging behavior, not the pet — the spec's central voice risk. The coverage lane frames them honestly as “here's why there's no signal yet,” which is literally B-053's mandate; it never competes with or dilutes a clinical finding, never spends a card-cap slot, and enriches exactly the thin no_pattern surface where Nyx lands today. The §5.2 “never co-render with staple_washout” rule already presumes the coverage lane. The one real cost is churn visibility (B2): churn fires only with active symptoms, and if a finding also fires the state isn't no_pattern, so the churn note stays silent then. That cost is acceptable because diet-structure is enrichment, not safety — the message is most needed precisely when the surface is otherwise empty. If the PM weighs churn-always-visible more heavily than the judgment/cap risk, Lane A is the alternative.

Candidate strings — Designer + nyx-voice ship-gate (blocking, §5.1)

Identical in either lane (only the renderer differs). Self-audited against nyx-voice Patterns 1–7 + clinical-guardrails: pet-named & second-person, specific counts with denominators, the non-negotiable log-only acknowledgement, never causal, never reassuring, no exclamation, never judgment. Final sign-off happens at build.

(a) Meal-type collapse

Card / why: “On 6 of the last 10 days, only treats were logged for Nyx — no meals. If that's the full picture of what she ate, it's worth sharing with your vet.” (coverage-lane why drops the trailing clause; the action carries the vet routing)
Sample line: “6 of 10 days · treats only”
Coverage action: “If that's the full picture, it's worth sharing with your vet. If she ate more than you logged, adding those meals helps us spot patterns.”
Honesty device (non-negotiable): the “if that matches what she actually ate” acknowledgement is present — the engine sees only the log and must not imply it knows what was eaten.

(b) Diet churn

Card: “3 new foods first appeared in Nyx's logs this week while her symptoms are active — each new food makes a pattern harder to spot.”
Sample line: “3 new foods this week · symptoms active”
Coverage why + action: “3 new foods first appeared in Nyx's logs this week — each new food makes it harder for us to tell what might be linked to the symptoms you're tracking.” + “Trying new foods is completely fine. If you're able to keep the diet steady for a stretch, patterns get easier to spot.”
Guardrail: suppressed on diet-trial pets (a vet-directed novel-protein switch is new food) — the “keep the diet steady” line must never contradict a vet's elimination trial (§5.2).
