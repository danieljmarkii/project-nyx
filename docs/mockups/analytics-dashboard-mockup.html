How colour carries the verdict — the inverted-polarity reference

The single most load-bearing visual decision (§11 #2/#3, open decision §13 #6). In a growth dashboard, green = up = good. For pet health that's backwards and quietly dangerous. Colour means “good or bad for the pet,” never “the number went up,” and it appears only on established, multi-sample metrics — a single observation stays neutral.
8↑ from 3
Adverse · rising
Vomiting climbing is going the wrong way → concern tone (muted rose). Clear, never alarm-red — we surface, we don't spike anxiety (Principle 4 / Dr. Chen).
3↓ from 8
Adverse · falling (shown in Frame 2)
Going the right way → a settled calm slate. Deliberately not green, not the accent: a dropping vomit count is relief, never a gamified “win,” never an all-clear. No streaks or badges on adverse metrics.
84%— meals finished
Descriptive · no verdict
Intake & rankings (top food/protein, finished-rate) get no good/bad colour at all. Colour here would imply a wellness judgement the data can't make — and high intake must never reassure (§11 #1).

The §11 safety invariants, made visible

InvariantWhere you see it in this mockup
#1 · Intake is not preferenceCard ⑥ titled “Meals finished,” never “Favourites.” Copy: “This is how much Nyx ate, not what she prefers.” A declining rate would route to a health watch (see notes), never “picky.”
#2 · n=1 never reassuresNo card wears a reassuring colour or read off a single sample. The falling-vomit card states the count + direction and stops — it never says “she's better.” Reassurance is reserved for the multi-sample AI summary (PR 4).
#3 · Adverse counts honest, never gamifiedCard ② — falling vomits = calm slate, not green; the sparkline is muted, the big number neutral. No “🎉”, no streak, no “win.”
#4 · Associational only, non-causal cueNo card asserts a cause. The Home Signal hedges (“since the switch”, “early pattern”); correlations live behind the engine with the explicit non-causal framing, not on these glance cards.
#5 · Minimum-sample floorsEvery ranking/rate shows its denominator (24 of… , 47 of 56). Below floor, the card becomes the “still learning Nyx's baseline” calibration state — never a fabricated rank (see notes).
#6 · Free-feeding honestyTop-food framed as “showed up in 24 meals” / “most-logged,” not “ate most” — logging ≠ observed intake; a free-fed food never reads as “didn't eat.”

Design notes & open calls for the team

Seeded, never build-your-own. The engine decides card priority (safety leads); the owner never composes a query, picks a segment, or builds a funnel. Same discipline as the Home Signal, extended (§3).
Empty & calibration states (§10) not shown as a frame. Each card owns a three-part empty state; below the sample floor it shows “still learning Nyx's baseline — 4 more days of meals and this card fills in,” never a flat line or a reassuring read on thin data. Worth a follow-up states board?
Range-free dashboard. No Week/Month/3-Month toggle on this surface — it's a glance. The toggle lives on each card's detail screen (the doorway chevron). Default range = Month (§13 #2).
Open · §13 #1 — user-facing name. “Nyx's patterns” is shown; leaning that or “Nyx's health.” Never “Analytics.” PM call.
Open · §13 #6 — the colour-as-wellness ruling (Data Scientist + Dr. Chen) gates the card component. This mockup proposes: colour only on established multi-sample adverse metrics; descriptive metrics stay neutral. Ratify before PR 2.
Open · ordering — diet-trial card position (§6 group A “leads” vs the prompt's last slot). Symptoms lead either way; the trial slot is the open call.
