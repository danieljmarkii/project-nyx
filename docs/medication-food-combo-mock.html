Slice C · the hero

The combo: log the treat, piggyback the pill

The owner's mental model is one act ("I gave her her treat with her pill"). A quiet, opt-in line on the treat's completion card lets them add the dose in a short detour — pick the drug, confirm. Be honest: engaging the combo is a few taps, not one (the picker in A2 is a real screen) — it's the slowest path on the card, justified by value, not speed. The 99% of meals with no medication tap nothing, and the card auto-dismisses — so Principle 1 stays intact for them. (Pet-owner review caveats below, §"What the pet owners flagged.")

Why it's not just convenience

The safety catch: a pill in a treat she didn't finish

This is the real reason to do it. When the pill rides inside the treat and Pixel doesn't finish it, the dose is in doubt. Two separate logs would happily record "treat: picked" and "dose: given" as unrelated facts — a false-adherence record, which the medication n=1-never-reassures rule forbids. Because the combo couples them, the card can ask honestly.

Slice B · build-first

The lightweight foundation: "how was it given?"

The cheap, always-correct piece that ships first — a single optional row on the medication completion card. It touches only the med path (zero meal-hot-path cost), captures the clinical "with food" fact on its own, and is the hook the combo reuses. Covers the common case: a pill in a dab of food you wouldn't bother logging as a meal.

What the pet owners flagged

Open questions from the Jordan + Sam review (2026-06-22)

The pet owners endorsed the direction — Flow C and Flow D are owner-validated — but pushed hard on two things (both of which the PM independently raised). These are revisit-time questions, not solved here.

⏱ Timing (the big one)
The safety prompt that justifies the whole feature lives on a card that auto-dismisses at 5s — and a chip tap replaces that with 1.5s, it doesn't extend it. But pilling a squirming cat and watching whether the vehicle goes down takes 20+ seconds, two hands. The moment Sam most needs the prompt is the moment she can't answer it in time → the safety prompt probably can't auto-dismiss at all (persist until answered, or fall back to a Home insight). Needs a live clickable prototype to judge.
✏️ Editing a combo
No after-the-fact story yet. The data model links a dose to a treat event, implying two separate History edits — but the owner's mental model is "one act" and expects to fix one thing. Designed answer required before build.
🐈 Ate around the pill
Cats lick the treat clean and leave the dry tablet. The treat reads "All" → the dose stays "Given" with no prompt → a false-adherence record in exactly the feline case the feature exists to prevent. Decide: detect-and-prompt, or document as a known limit.
🔎 Discoverability
A quiet line on a 5s card is easy to never notice — fine for Jordan (skips it), risky for Sam (needs it daily). Resolve only with a prototype: "did Sam find it unprompted?"
🐾 Multi-pet
The dose inherits the pet from the treat event; in a shared-bowl household a wrong-pet dose is a real adherence error. Show active-pet context on the combo card.

Verdicts — Flow C (how_given): WORKS-FOR-ME (build first) · Flow D (trap): WORKS-FOR-ME · Flow A (combo) + Flow B (safety): NEEDS-WORK (timing + edit model + a live prototype). Full write-up: docs/medication-food-combo-investigation.md §9.
