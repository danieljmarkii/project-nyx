The crescent is the night — calm, premium, the equity we already own. The single teal dot is the Signal: the one thing the app found in the noise. The one thing that did it. Moon & Signal is the team's aligned direction — one mark that is both the moon and the culprit.

The wordmark shares the atom

The crescent becomes the C; the dot over the i is the same teal Signal. One brand atom lives in the icon, the wordmark, and — literally — in the product's Signal engine.

ulprıt

Shown in a system serif standing in for Newsreader, our locked display face. Body/labels stay Geist. The lockup speaks the type system we already ship — no new typeface to adopt (unlike the Turn 4 routes).

A colour world, not just an accent

Teal alone gives us a mark. Adding midnight indigo as a brand-night gives us a world to market with — night sky plus one point of light. Indigo is additive and does not break the design system, because it is a background colour, never an interactive one.

The rule that keeps the system intact

Teal stays the single interactive accent — buttons, the trend line, live state. Indigo is a world colour — grounds, heroes, the icon field. Never a tappable accent. The "one accent, never decorative" rule survives.

Guardrail

Red stays off the brand. #F43F5E already means symptom in-app (and #DC2626 means destructive). Every red-forward Turn 4 mark would read faintly as a warning. We keep teal as the "gotcha".

Beyond the icon

A great icon that can't extend isn't a brand. The dot scales up into hero art and store presence; the Whorl — the crescent drawn as a fingerprint — is the secondary "whodunit" texture the icon never has to carry.

Why the team landed here

01

It says what the app does

The only mark in 24 that encodes the Signal — our actual differentiator. The dot is the one finding, the culprit, caught.

02

It's distinct in a moon-crowded category

Calm, Sleep Cycle and friends own the plain crescent. The teal point of light is what makes ours un-mistakable in the grid.

03

It bridges the fork

Keeps the moon's calm equity (Jordan, Sam, Dr. Chen) and answers the name (the culprit) — without the cost of picking a pole.

04

It's a system, not a logo

One atom recurs across icon, wordmark, colour world, and product. That coherence is what makes marketing cheap and consistent.

05

It's an evolution, not a reset

Keeps teal and the incumbent moon; adds indigo. No new typeface, no abandoned equity — unlike every Turn 4 route.

Held in reserve

Two options we deliberately keep on the table as the direction is tightened.

Safe fallback · 2a

Classic Teal Badge

If the indigo path needs more bake time, the white-crescent-on-teal badge ships today with zero brand risk and maximum legibility. It de-risks the App-Store deadline.

Strategic alternate · 4f

The Verdict

The one to pressure-test if we ever decide to lead with the name over the moon. Grown-up, editorial, distinct — but it abandons teal and needs a brand accent that isn't the symptom red.

To tighten before it's final
