Culprit · In-App Brand Direction

The night
comes inside.

The icon promises the moon that finds the one thing in the dark. This is the designer's pass at keeping that promise on real screens: one rule for where the night world lives in-app, the Whorl as a working loading system, a living brand mark, the Patterns calendar redesigned, and a Home that briefs instead of stacking three fixed boxes.

Sr. Product Designer · 2026-07-09 · every mock below is interactive & indicative — a spec to ratify, not final art

The organizing idea

Night is when Culprit thinks

Not "add dark mode." One principled rule for where the night world appears: whenever the app is working on the pet's behalf — finding, analyzing, building — the surface is night. Whenever the owner is capturing or reading records, the surface stays the calm, clinical day system we shipped. The 3am log stays light, fast, and boring on purpose; the intelligence gets the sky it was drawn in.

Night — the app thinking

Indigo world, aurora glow, moonlight serif, the Signal dot.

  • The Signal zone on Home — what the moon found
  • The Patterns AI summary — same voice, same sky
  • Loading beyond a beat — cold start, report build, photo reads
  • The unauthenticated landing — the brand's front door

Day — the owner acting

The shipped light system, untouched: calm, glanceable, 10-second fast.

  • Quick-log — zero decisions at the moment of event
  • History, Foods, Profile — records read like records
  • Patterns cards — deterministic numbers on white
  • The vet report — clinical-grade, no decoration, out of scope here
The system survives intact: teal stays the only tappable accent in both registers; indigo is only ever a ground (never a button, never a trend line); symptom rose keeps meaning symptom — on night grounds it lifts one step to #FB7185 for contrast (6.8:1), the same move the photo viewer already makes with colorDestructiveOnDark.
Proposal 1 · the C and the dot, in the app

The mark lives in the header — and the dot is a status light

Home's header currently says "Culprit" in quiet gray text. It becomes the real lockup — crescent-C plus the teal tittle. The dot you loved pulsing on the brand page earns its motion here: it breathes only when the Signal has something new you haven't seen, and goes still the moment you look. Quiet by default; alive exactly when the product has done its job.

Culprit
N Nyx Cat · 4 yrs · diet trial, day 12

How it behaves

  • Quiet state (default): static dot. The mark is identity, not a banner — the header stays subordinate to the Signal headline (Principle 3).
  • Fresh-signal state: the dot breathes with one soft ping ring — teal-as-live-state, which the one-accent rule explicitly sanctions. Tapping it scrolls to the Signal; the pulse stops once the finding is seen.
  • Never for safety: a safety finding leads the Signal zone itself with its own rail — the header dot is a gentle "something new," not an alarm channel.
  • Same component everywhere: replaces the generic lucide Moon in AuthBrandMark and the text-only header wordmark — one CulpritMark, one carve rule.

Reduced-motion: the dot holds a static soft glow; the ping ring never renders. Engineering: react-native-svg + native-driver scale/opacity — both already in the stack.

Proposal 3 · the marquee move

The Signal card is the night sky

The Signal is the product's namesake — the one thing found in the dark — yet it renders as one more white card. Proposal: it becomes the single night surface on Home. The aurora you loved is its ground, the headline wears moonlight Newsreader, and the found thing gets a literal Signal dot. Everything else on Home stays day — the contrast is the point: one piece of sky in a calm, light room.

Proposed Home — tap the states above

What changed, what didn't

  • Ground only: indigo is the card's world; every tappable thing on it is still teal. The "one accent" rule survives by construction.
  • Safety stays legible: the safety rail and its tag lift to #FB7185 on night (6.8:1 — vs 4.98:1 for the day rose). Ranking, phrasing, and never-reassure guardrails are untouched — this is paint, not logic.
  • The lead finding keeps Newsreader — now in moonlight on the sky it was designed for. Secondary findings stay in the body face, exactly like today.
  • Empty states become features (Principle 5): "building" gets the mini-Whorl doing visible work; "quiet night" gets the moon itself — honest copy, no all-clear.
  • The conservative alternate (if the full night card feels heavy on-device): keep the white card, give only the "Signal" label row the night treatment as a thin band. It's reversible either way — same content, two grounds.

Watch-out we'll verify on-device: a dark card on a light screen must read as the intelligence surface, not an ad slot — the section label, identical typography, and zero CTA styling are what keep it editorial. Jordan test: nothing about the 10-second log path changes.

Proposal 4 · beyond three boxes

Home becomes a briefing, not a fixed layout

Today Home is three hard-coded zones. The principle ("a curated, prioritized set of insight cards") never demanded three — the layout did. Proposal: cards appear only when they have something to say, in a strict priority order, and disappear when they don't. Still curated, still capped by relevance, still never a feed — but a pet on two medications with a diet trial running gets a fuller briefing than a healthy pet on kibble.

The briefing rules (what keeps this from becoming a feed)

  • Fixed priority order, content-gated: cross-pet safety → Signal → Today → Care due → Diet trial → Trend → Weight. A card renders only when it carries information today — no meds, no Care card; no trial, no trial card.
  • Safety is never dropped to honor any cap — unchanged from the revised Principle 3.
  • Every card is a doorway to a real surface (History, the regimen, Patterns) — never a dead end, never an upsell.
  • Care due is intelligence, not a nag: it states a fact about today's regimen with a one-tap log — it appears at most once and never pushes.

This is a Tier-2 change: the three-zone text lives in design-principles.md §3, so adopting the briefing model is a PM ratification, not a silent edit. Shown with everything firing — a healthy, unmedicated pet sees exactly today's three zones.

Proposal 5 · Patterns, decoded (B-226)

Count the dots, not the shades

Your calendar disappointment is a real encoding bug: opacity ramps ask people to measure a shade; nobody can. Episode counts are 1, 2, 3 — countable things get countable marks. Each day shows one dot per episode. And the card grows what B-226 asked for: real month paging and a tap on any day that shows everything logged that day, not just the card's symptom. The three B-226 complaints, answered

① Day drill-inTap a day, see the whole dayEvery logged event that day — meals, doses, symptoms — with times. The card is scoped to one symptom; the day is not.
② Month navigationTrue paginated months‹ › steps calendar months; the current month is the default and the future is disabled. Swipe on-device.
③ Encoding rethinkPips replace the shade ramp1–3 episodes = 1–3 dots; 4+ = a compact count. Exact at a glance, no legend decoding, colorblind-safe by construction (count, not hue-step).

Dot color stays the one symptom rose — it marks status, never magnitude. Days keep ≥44pt touch targets at real scale. The old opacity ramp below, for the receipt:

The Patterns AI summary (top of the phone) joins the night family — it's the same voice as the Signal, so it wears the same sky. The deterministic cards below it stay day-white: numbers read like records.

Proposal 6 · the smallest, most-felt one

Every log lands like a signal

The log-completion moment already springs a mint check. One addition: a single teal ping ring — the same gesture as the icon's dot — ripples once as the check lands. Every data point the owner gives us is a future signal; now saving one feels like it. The gold celebrate glow stays for the moments that earn it.

Logged

Why this one matters most

  • The quick-log is the single most-repeated act in the app — the brand atom lands exactly where the data lands, ~1,000 times a year for an active owner.
  • One ripple, ~700ms, native-driver scale + opacity — inside the existing CompletionMoment animation budget. Symptom logs keep the calm tone: check + ping, no gold, no festivity.
  • Reduced-motion: the ping never renders; the check simply appears.
