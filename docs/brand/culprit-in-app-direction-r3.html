The rule, in full: findings present → the night-sky card (any priority); safety findings additionally wear the design system's danger styling (rail + tag); no established findings → a plain light card with plain copy. Building (first days) → the light card with the mini-Whorl and ghost previews. That's the whole spec — nothing varies by mood, only by what the engine found.

Why this answers all three rounds at once

  • Round 1's "direction genuinely good": the night-sky homage survives untouched — same ground, same stars, same moonlight Newsreader lead.
  • Round 1's "contrast too high": resolved by presence, not by softening. Most days, most pets — no dark card at all. When one appears, it has earned the attention it draws.
  • Round 2's "hate it": all three page-treatment compromises are deleted. The page never changes; only the card's content-state does.
  • Your danger-styling note: nothing new invented — the rose rail and tag are the shipped design system's safety treatment, lifted to #FB7185 on the dark ground for contrast (6.8:1), same precedent as colorDestructiveOnDark.
  • The "quiet night" language is dead: the empty state says No established patterns yet and explains the evidence bar in one breath. Honest, plain, no poetry, no all-clear.

A glance-value bonus worth naming plainly: because the dark card only renders with findings, dark-on-Home becomes glanceable information. And the header mark's pulse (locked in round 2) composes with it — the dot breathes, you open the app, the sky is there with the reason why.
