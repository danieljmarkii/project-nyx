Making protein exposure scannable in 60 seconds

Three chart treatments of the same 90-day off-diet feeding record (Apr 6 – Jul 4), drafted before we build the render path into the vet report. The clinical story is that chicken accounts for roughly three-quarters of every off-diet exposure, so no single protein can be isolated as a trigger — a chart should make that obvious at a glance rather than buried in a table.

The one tension resolved here: the report is printed and photocopied in black & white (house rule §5.8, no load-bearing colour). A seven-category stack can't lean on hue alone, so every protein carries both a colourblind-safe hue and a distinct SVG texture — the segments stay separable in colour and in greyscale. A greyscale proof is included below.

Why texture, not just colour. Segments are filled with SVG <pattern> textures — chicken is solid, turkey a diagonal hatch, tuna a cross-hatch, lobster dots, whitefish horizontal rules, rabbit vertical rules, “no protein” a flat pale grey. The hue is a colourblind-safe accent; the texture is what survives a black-and-white photocopy. The legend carries hue + texture + label + count so it decodes either way. Empty weeks (Apr 6 – May 4) are honest — off-diet logging began mid-May.

Option B · same data, composition

Share of exposure & protein ranking

The identical 354 exposures collapsed to “who dominates” — a 100%-width share bar over a ranked total bar. Tradeoff: drops all timing information, but answers the vet's actual first question — “is one protein driving this?” — in a single glance, and gives the thin proteins a readable length.

Clinical caution. This view co-plots symptom load and exposure volume, which reads as a correlation the data can't earn (n=1, chicken confounds everything). If it ships at all it belongs owner-side, or under an explicit “association, not cause” note — never as a standalone claim on the vet report.
Data. Real Nyx 90-day window, Apr 6 – Jul 4 2026 (cat; chronic intermittent vomiting). 354 off-diet exposures = 347 treats + 7 human-food feedings, plus ~28 rated Instinct wet meals (not counted as off-diet). Protein totals: chicken 274, no-recorded-protein 27, turkey 18, tuna 16, lobster 10, ocean whitefish 6, rabbit 3. Vomiting: 22 episodes across 19 days. Weekly bins spread each treat item evenly across its logged date span — plausible for a mock, not exact per-day counts.
Build notes. Fully self-contained for the report's strict CSP: all CSS inline, all charts inline <svg>, textures as reusable <pattern> paint servers — no scripts, fonts, CDNs, chart libraries, or remote assets. System sans-serif, tabular figures, hairline #e4e5e8 gridlines, zero-baseline axes.
