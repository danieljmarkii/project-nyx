Culprit · Product design

A home for the owner, not just the pet

The Pet tab quietly does two jobs today: it holds pet data (conditions, medications, weight, the vet report) and, jammed at the bottom, account actions that have nowhere else to live — your name, sign out, delete account. This proposal pulls those user actions into a screen of their own — You — and gives the support email and legal links the home four App-Store items are already waiting on.

Decided this round (PM, 2026-07-09). Entry point ✓ avatar in the Home header (a doorway, not a fifth tab). Screen mock ✓ approved. Name ✓ "You" — the squad's pick over "Profile," which in a pet-first app collides with the pet's own profile. The alternatives are kept below for the record.

Why this is worth doing now

Four submission items are waiting on a screen that doesn't exist

None of these has anywhere to point today. Build the screen once and each drops into place the moment its URL goes live — this sits on the App-Store critical path, not beside it.

Signing out and deleting your account are not pet actions.

A rescue owner looking for "how do I contact support" or "where's the privacy policy" has no reason to look under a tab named Pet — and there's nothing there to find. The fix is to give the owner their own place.

Decision 1 · The entry point

How the owner reaches it

The design constitution reserves a nav tab for "a destination you return to and act within," and lists a settings shortcut under what is not on the home screen. A low-frequency account screen wants a doorway, not a tab — so a quiet avatar in the header chrome (not among the Signal cards) is the benchmark move Oura, Calm and Linear all make.

The question · what else belongs here

Room to grow, without getting loud

The screen should scale to hold more without becoming a firehose. The way it stays calm is structure: as rows accrue, group them under quiet labels — Account · Preferences · Your data · Support · About — so it always reads at a glance. Here's the menu the squad would pull from.

In v1 — approved this round

Account (name + email) · Notifications (mocked) · Contact support · Share feedback (mocked) · Privacy / Terms / disclaimer · Sign out + Delete account (moved off the Pet tab) · app version.
